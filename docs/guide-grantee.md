# Sharing and Reusing Your Data with the Philanthropy Data Commons

*A guide for grantees and changemakers applying through the MacArthur Foundation portal.*

This guide is written for the people filling out a grant application in the
MacArthur Foundation portal — you do not need any technical background to follow
it. It explains what the Philanthropy Data Commons (PDC) is, how to consent to
sharing your data, and how to reuse data you have already submitted so you don't
have to re-type it on every application.

---

## What is the Philanthropy Data Commons?

The Philanthropy Data Commons (PDC) is shared data infrastructure for the
philanthropic sector. Its goal is to **reduce the burden of filling out new
grant applications**: instead of re-entering the same organization details and
answers for every funder, you enter them once and reuse them.

You can learn more at [philanthropydatacommons.org](https://philanthropydatacommons.org/).

There are two things you can do through the MacArthur portal:

1. **Contribute** the data from an application to the PDC (with your consent).
2. **Reuse** data you've already contributed to auto-fill a new application.

Both are optional. Your participation in the PDC is not reviewed during grantmaking 
descisions.

---

## Part 1 — Consenting to share your data

Your application data is **only** shared with the PDC if you actively consent.
Nothing is shared automatically.

### How consent works

- While editing your application form you'll see a short explainer titled
  **"Contribute to Philanthropy Data Commons"** and a link to a consent section.
- The consent language explains that you are giving the Foundation permission to
  share information about **this proposal and your organization** with the PDC,
  to be used under the PDC's governing data use agreement.
- To consent, check the consent box. By checking it you confirm that you are
  authorized to consent on behalf of your organization.

### Things worth knowing before you consent

- **Consent is per-proposal but carried forward to new applications.** A decision 
  you make on this application does not change any decision you made on a past grant.
  A decision today will be automatically applied to new applications created from today
  forward.
- **Sharing is not instant.** In the MacArthur portal your data is NOT sent to
  the PDC the moment you submit unless the record was already granted. Once the 
  application reaches a granted state, it will be sent to the PDC.
- **You can review exactly what will be shared.** The consent window shows a preview
  of the specific fields that would be sent to the PDC, so there are no
  surprises.

If you have questions about the consent itself, the consent text links to a
Q&A page and to `legal@macfound.org`.

## Part 2 — Creating a PDC account

To reuse data already submitted to the PDC, you need your own account with the PDC 
(separate from your MacArthur portal login).

1. Go to [philanthropydatacommons.org](https://www.philanthropydatacommons.org)
   and reach out to the PDC to create an account.
2. Use that account to log in when you want to access or reuse data you've
   submitted.

> **Note:** Because data isn't sent immediately after you submit an application,
> there may be nothing to see the very first time you log in. That's expected —
> check back later.

## Part 3 — Reusing your data with the Data Explorer

Once you have data in the PDC, you can pull it into a new application on any
funder that uses this feature — including MacArthur.

### Step by step

1. **Open the application form** for the funder you're applying to.
2. **Log in to the PDC.** A secure PDC login window opens. Sign in with your PDC
   account. (You log in directly with the PDC — the portal never sees your PDC
   password.)
3. **Launch the Data Explorer.** A panel appears on the form for connecting to
   the PDC.
4. **Review your prior submissions.** Once connected, the Data Explorer shows
   proposals and answers you've already submitted to the PDC.
5. **Populate the form.** You can either:
   - **Auto-populate** matching fields all at once, or
   - **Drag and drop** an individual value from the panel onto the field you
     want it in.
6. **Review and edit.** Filled-in values are a starting point. Read them over,
   edit anything that needs updating for this specific application, and complete
   any fields the PDC didn't have data for.
7. **Submit as usual.** Once you're happy with the form, use the Submit button.

### Good to know

- Auto-filled answers are **suggestions from your own past submissions** — always
  review them before submitting, especially for anything time-sensitive.
- The Data Explorer only reads your data to help you fill the form. Sharing new
  data with the PDC still happens only through the consent step in Part 1.

## Quick reference

| I want to… | Do this |
|---|---|
| Learn what the PDC is | Open the "What's the Philanthropy Data Commons?" explainer on the form, or visit philanthropydatacommons.org |
| Share this application's data | Review and check the consent box on the form |
| See what will be shared | Review the field preview shown with the consent section |
| Get a PDC account | Contact the PDC via philanthropydatacommons.org |
| Reuse past data | Log in to the PDC and launch the Data Explorer. Click auto-populate or drag values onto the form |
| Ask about consent | See the Q&A linked in the consent text or email legal@macfound.org |

## Frequently asked questions

**Do I have to use the PDC to apply for a grant?**
No. Participation is entirely optional. Your application works exactly the same
whether or not you consent to sharing or reuse any data.

**Will my data be shared if I don't check the consent box?**
No. Data is shared only if you actively consent.

**I consented but I don't see my data in the PDC yet. Is something wrong?**
Probably not. Data is not sent immediately on submission and can take time to
appear. Check back later.

**Does consenting on this grant change my past grants?**
No. Each consent decision applies to its own proposal. Prior consents are
unaffected but future consent will carry forward from the previous application.

**Is my PDC login safe?**
Yes. You log in directly with the PDC in its own secure window. The MacArthur
portal never receives or stores your PDC password.
